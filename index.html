Why Mexico City?

Mexico City, a vibrant metropolis that now stands as the capital of Mexico, has a rich and multifaceted history that traces its origins to ancient times. The city's foundation can be attributed to the Aztecs, who established their magnificent capital of Tenochtitlan on an island in the middle of Lake Texcoco in the 14th century. Characterized by intricate networks of canals, floating gardens, and awe-inspiring pyramids, Tenochtitlan thrived as a cultural and political hub until its conquest by Spanish forces led by Hernán Cortés in 1521.

The Spanish colonial era saw the city's transformation into a European-style urban center, with grand cathedrals, palaces, and plazas taking root amidst the remnants of Aztec marvels. Over the centuries, Mexico City evolved through various phases, grappling with challenges such as urbanization, social upheaval, and cultural fusion. Today, it stands as a bustling amalgamation of history and modernity, where ancient civilizations' legacy intertwines with contemporary dynamism.

My favorite cafés in Mexico City

Cicatriz Cafe

Cicatriz Cafe in Mexico City is a renowned culinary oasis that captivates locals and visitors alike with its unique blend of flavors and ambiance. Nestled in the heart of the city, the cafe offers a diverse menu that showcases the rich culinary heritage of Mexico while embracing international influences. With its chic decor, inviting atmosphere, and expertly crafted coffee and dishes, Cicatriz Cafe stands as a must-visit destination for those seeking an authentic and memorable dining experience in Mexico City.

Address: Dinamarca 44 Juarez CDMX 06600

Panaderia Rosetta

The bakery offers a diverse selection of bread, including sourdough, baguettes, and rustic loaves, all crafted with attention to detail and a focus on natural fermentation. In addition to bread, Panadería Rosetta is celebrated for its delectable pastries, such as croissants, danishes, and tarts, which showcase a balance of classic European techniques with a Mexican twist.

Address: Colima 179, Roma Nte., Cuauhtémoc, 06700 Ciudad de México, CDMX, Mexico
